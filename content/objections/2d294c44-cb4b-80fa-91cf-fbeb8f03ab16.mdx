<Toggle title="Overall Summary" level={2}>

Based on the available evidence and data, DM proved to be highly accurate in the following areas: Generation of 3D models (SmartSTL) that proved to be consistently accurate enough for clinical applications with an accuracy similar to IOS-generated 3D models. DM’s AI also proved to be highly accurate in detecting common clinical incidents and emergencies such as Bracket debonding, tie loss, open self-ligating clips, aligner tracking issues, loss of attachments & buttons. Some early studies suggest that DM is accurate enough to detect occlusion corrections such as canine class 1, overjet, overbite, crossbite, etc. Where DM’s AI shows some weaknesses is in hygiene issues detection. While it has low to moderate sensitivity (false negatives), it has high specificity (no false alerts). However, other studies have shown that regardless of the accuracy, DM consistently improves oral hygiene.

## Evidence

<Toggle title="**1. AI Accuracy for 3D Model Reconstruction and Tooth Movement Tracking (SmartSTL)**" level={2}>

Multiple studies confirm DM AI's ability to create clinically reliable 3D models and accurately track tooth movement, validating its equivalence to in-office scanners.

- **Clinically Equivalent to IOS-generated 3D Models:** Comparative studies have shown **no clinically significant difference** (defined as greater than ±0.5 mm) between DM-generated 3D digital models (SmartSTL) and gold-standard intraoral scans (iTero Element 5D) - [**Homsi K et al.**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/17AWW6YnTFiwYPpuO11mpZTtRh6FQOeFS/view?usp=sharing)**, **[**Morris RS et al.**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1S2b-8lxBPmtHyQOPcveUsgWeARMZtUgo/view?usp=sharing)
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-3.jpg)

- **Precision in Transverse Measurements:** Comparisons of transverse dental measurements (inter-canine, inter-premolar, and inter-molar) on AI-generated models showed mean differences ranging from 0.05 mm to 0.40 mm, which is **below the 0.5 mm clinical acceptability threshold - **[**Pasciuti E et al.**](https://www.mdpi.com/3421832)
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-4.png)

- **High Concordance for Movement Parameters:** A pilot study found that the mean deviations between AI-generated and intraoral scan models were **below 0.5° for angular movements** and **below 0.15 mm for linear movements**, demonstrating sufficient accuracy for clinical use in aligner treatment - [Vassis S et al.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40984693/)
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-5.jpg)

- **Accurate Refinement STLs:** In a case report, a remotely created Smart STL file matched a conventional intraoral scan with a mean deviation of **0.08 mm**, supporting the reliability of the AI for generating refinement files - [**Perez Varela JC, et al.**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1bCrlUJ96EwMfFU2JJqwa-9P1FacSkA27/view?usp=sharing)
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-6.png)

</Toggle>

<Toggle title="**2. AI Diagnostic Accuracy for Common Clinical Incidents/Emergencies**" level={2}>

The AI demonstrates extremely high diagnostic accuracy, often exceeding 90% sensitivity and specificity, in detecting critical issues remotely.

- **Aligners Auxiliary Loss Detection:** A multi-centric retrospective study validated the AI's performance in detecting the loss of aligner auxiliaries with extremely high diagnostic accuracy.
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-7.png)

  *(A) Loss of attachment on the maxillary right second premolar (UR5), indicated by a yellow arrow. (B) Missing buttons on the mandibular left first and second premolars (LL4 and LL5).*


  - **Attachment loss:** Sensitivity 98.2%, Specificity 100%.
  - **Button loss:** Sensitivity 98.4%, Specificity 99.0%.
  [**McCray JF et al.**](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1073874625001008)


- **Braces Incidents:** A multi-centric retrospective study validated the AI's performance in detecting the loss of brackets, ties, and open self-ligating clips
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-8.png)

  *(A) Bracket Debonding UL4, (B) Loss of Tie LR5, (C) Open Clip UR1*


  - **Bracket debonding:** Sensitivity 98.4%, Specificity 99.6%.
  - **Tie loss:** Sensitivity 93.3%, Specificity 96.5%.
  - **Open self-ligating clips**: Sensitivity 91.1%, Specificity 88.3%.

  [**McCray JF et al.**](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-37329-w)

- **Aligner Tracking Issues:** The AI has a proven ability to detect and differentiate between slight versus noticeable aligner unseats with high metrics.
  ![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-9.png)

  - Two-level detection (seat vs unseat): Sensitivity 93.2%, Specificity 86.2% - [**McCray JF et al.**](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41501754/)
- **Archwire Passivity:** A prospective study found the AI to have **high accuracy** in detecting the absence of tooth movement (passivity) of archwires and auxiliaries during fixed treatment, with a Sensitivity of 90.4% and Specificity of 85.5% - **Publication in process, FDA Approved**
- **Hygiene issues detection:** According to the available literature, DM demonstrates **moderate accuracy** in detecting oral hygiene–related clinical findings. In one study evaluating AI-based detection of plaque or calculus, gingivitis, and gingival recession, the sensitivity values were 53%, 35%, and 22%, respectively, while specificity remained high at 94%, 96%, and 99% ([**Snider et al., 2024**](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38363256/)). These results indicate that although DM is likely to miss some early or mild hygiene issues (lower sensitivity), it rarely produces false positives (high specificity), meaning false alerts to patients or practices are unlikely. Some observational reports also suggest that the system may have difficulty distinguishing slight from more advanced hygiene concerns; however, this limitation can be mitigated by disabling notifications for minor findings.

Importantly, several clinical studies have demonstrated that **regardless of detection sensitivity**, the use of DM consistently leads to **significant improvements in patient oral hygiene over time**, particularly during fixed-appliance therapy where hygiene challenges are common ([**Snider et al., 2023**](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37113065/)**; **[**You et al., 2024**](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39549249/)).
</Toggle>

<Toggle title="**3. AI Accuracy in Occlusion Assessment **" level={2}>

The AI is validated for assessing occlusal parameters and ensuring high-quality patient-generated data.

- **Occlusal Assessment:** A quantitative study validating the AI's accuracy in detecting malocclusions (midline deviation, overbite, overjet, and canine relationship) found that the **mean bias across all parameters was less than 0.5 mm**, confirming its reliability as a remote diagnosis and triage tool.
  **Publication in process, FDA Approved**

- **Expansion and crossbite correction:** A study on the assessment of rapid palatal expansion using Dental Monitoring (DM) software found that DM accurately assessed the correction of posterior crossbite and showed no significant difference in intermolar width measurements compared to digital models or intraoral examinations - [**Kuriakose P et al.**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1AvIoIofB7kYDaA0SFxAkWdcmK1ivdLJW/view?usp=sharing)
</Toggle>

<Toggle title="**4. DM Scan Accuracy & Quality**" level={2}>

![](/images/objections/2d294c44-cb4b-80fa-91cf-fbeb8f03ab16/image-10.png)

- **Reliable Photo Acquisition:** There was **98.9% agreement** between the DM-generated photosets and live clinical evaluations, demonstrating the system's accuracy/validity in clinical photo acquisition - [**Elnagar MH et al.**](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S221244382500791X)
- **Image Quality:** Studies have shown that there is **no difference in image quality** between the AI-powered scans taken by patients versus those taken by the clinician - [**Moylan HB et al.**](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8111833/)
