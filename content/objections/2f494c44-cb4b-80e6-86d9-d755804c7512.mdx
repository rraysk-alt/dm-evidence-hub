Overall summary of the studies

</Callout>

![](/images/objections/2f494c44-cb4b-80e6-86d9-d755804c7512/image-2.png)

<Callout emoji="💡">

**A global DM study including 2248 patients**** [**[**Study link here**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1lLadMLKL0cZZZ72eiCXhkCpTZzI8T7q5/view?usp=sharing)**]**

</Callout>

- 86% of patients were globally satisfied with their remote monitoring experience.
- 89% of patients found it very beneficial to be able to communicate with their orthodontist via the DentalMonitoring (DM) app.
- 86% of patients felt more reassured by being constantly monitored.
- 88% of patients found DM easy to use.
- 76% of respondents felt more engaged and compliant, with a positive impact on hygiene.
- The most mentioned benefits were "more convenience due to fewer visits," "better communication," and "reassurance".
- Patients rated their experience an average of 8.57 out of 10.
- DM achieved a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 51.5, which is considered "excellent".
- 85% of patients said it would be beneficial to use DM in the post-treatment phase.
<Callout emoji="💡">

Dalessandri et al., a peer-reviewed study about patients’ attitudes towards DM [[**Study link here**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1rnv05G61HVLXNfTrqTQ9-c4317ODPs9b/view?usp=sharing)]

</Callout>

- **Positive Judgement:** 97.5% of patients judged DM positively.
- **High-Tech Perception:** 81.25% of patients considered telemonitoring indicative of high-tech treatment
- **Interest in Reducing In-Office Visits:** 81.25% of patients declared to be interested in reducing the number of in-office visits through telemonitoring
- **High-Quality Treatment Perception:** 49 patients (81.7%) considered telemonitoring indicative of high-quality treatment
<Callout emoji="💡">

Hansa et al., peer-reviewed study published in Seminars in Orthodontics [[**Study link here**](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1073874618300690)]

</Callout>

- 86% of the patients found the DM app “easy” or “very easy” to use 
- 84% of the patients found the DM app to be “beneficial” or “very beneficial”
- The most oft-mentioned perceived benefits were “better communication” (47 times), “increased convenience” (44 times), “reduced number of appointments” (40 times), and “ease of use” (38 times).
<Callout emoji="💡">

Gil-Ortega et al., a peer-reviewed study published in Nature, pediatric patient experience with DM [[**Study link here**](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-24395-9)]

</Callout>

- 100% were satisfied or very satisfied with the DM application overall and with communication with the orthodontist.
- 85% found the scanning process very easy or easy.
- 85% would recommend the experience.
- 55% felt much calmer knowing they were continuously monitored.
