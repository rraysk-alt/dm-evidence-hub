- **Learning how to manage the system and fear of increased workload** are the most common reported concerns before implementation, but these quickly change over time.
![](/images/objections/2d394c44-cb4b-8042-940b-c684d3c815f2/image-3.png)

- **Perception Improved Significantly Over Time:** Initial concerns about DM fade significantly with experience. The median perception score increased from 6/10 before implementation to 8/10 just three months later, and by the time of the survey, the mean score was 9.1, with 100% of responses at 8 or above.
![](/images/objections/2d394c44-cb4b-8042-940b-c684d3c815f2/image-4.png)

- **Enhanced Professional Role and Purpose:** DM enables the role of the orthodontic assistant to evolve toward a more empowered and valued position. DMCs reported strong positive impacts on professional growth (94%), work autonomy (83%), and work purpose (77%).
![](/images/objections/2d394c44-cb4b-8042-940b-c684d3c815f2/image-5.png)

- **Familiarity Boosts Positivity:** Longer experience with DM was associated with higher satisfaction and a stronger sense of contribution. Furthermore, longer DM experience correlated significantly with a more positive current perception of the platform and a stronger sense that their work is valuable.
- **Effective Training and Support:** A large majority of respondents (88%) rated the training they received from the DM team as positive, suggesting that adequate onboarding helps build confidence and reduces friction. 
![](/images/objections/2d394c44-cb4b-8042-940b-c684d3c815f2/image-6.png)


> **Addressing the "DM takes too much time to manage” objection**

- The median daily time spent managing DM was approximately 25 minutes per 100 patients, and 60% of DMCs spent 30 minutes or less per day per 100 patients, even when managing large caseloads.
![](/images/objections/2d394c44-cb4b-8042-940b-c684d3c815f2/image-7.png)

<Callout emoji="💡">

[****[Explore the study here]****](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ydIhxIzI-gnE1db3psILoCJdL2eXsZeg/view?usp=sharing)

</Callout>

- In a study published in the AJODO in 2021, the authors stated that, on average, managing the DM dashboard required 4 to 5 hours a week for 275 active patients. This was mostly managed by the staff, or a designated Dental Monitoring Coordinator. It took the orthodontist around 30 min a week. **This number translates to 1 minute per patient per week for the staff and a few seconds for the orthodontist.**
<Callout emoji="💡">

[****[Explore Dr. Janka Kochel’s study here]****](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1roTX2mQ1nWxIsjdkfkEomtMIkFZQex5U/view?usp=sharing)

</Callout>

In a recent analysis done by Dr. Janka Kochel in her private practice in Germany, she found similar numbers: **45 minutes per day for 250 active patients, while she spent around 5 to 10 minutes daily on the dashboard.**

So in a 50-week work year, that number added up to around 190 hours.

Dr. Kochel also calculated how much chair time was saved because of DM and that number added up to around 209 hours. A little bit more than was spent managing DM.
